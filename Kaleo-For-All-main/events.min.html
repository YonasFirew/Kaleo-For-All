Timket commemorates the baptism of Jesus in the Jordan River by John the Baptist. This festival is one of the most colorful and vibrant celebrations in Ethiopia, symbolizing spiritual purification and renewal. The faithful gather at water sources to participate in the blessing of water, symbolizing the baptism of Christ and the renewal of their own spiritual lives.

Meskel marks the finding of the True Cross by Queen Helena (Empress Helena), the mother of Emperor Constantine. According to tradition, she discovered the cross on which Jesus was crucified. This celebration is a testament to the power of faith and the victory of light over darkness, truth over falsehood.

üî• Spirit of Celebration

Bonfires (Demera) are lit, symbolizing light defeating darkness. The celebration begins with the building of a large bonfire, which is then lit as a symbol of the discovery of the True Cross. People gather around the fire, singing hymns and prayers, creating a powerful sense of community and shared faith. The ashes from the fire are used to mark crosses on foreheads as a blessing.

Fasika is the celebration of Jesus Christ's resurrection after His crucifixion. The resurrection is the foundation of Christian faith, demonstrating God's power and love for humanity.

üåÖ Spirit of Celebration

The atmosphere is filled with hymns of praise and thanksgiving.
